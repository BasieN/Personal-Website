So I Asked AI To Create Me...

As part of a trend that was going around, I asked ChatGPT to generate a caricature of me based on everything it had learnt about me.
It pulled from my interests — AI, Unity, cybersecurity, tutoring, tech society, coding, research, building cool things, etc. — and stitched all of that context together.
And then it gave me... this guy →
Not the black woman that I actually am — but apparently a smiling, bearded tech bro.
This, according to ChatGPT, is how it sees me.

Curious, I asked why it assumed I looked like that. The explanation was... interesting:

"Short answer: I filled in a visual blank with a stereotype. Most of the signals I had about you were role-based (software engineering, AI, game dev, tutoring in tech, etc.). Because tech is so often incorrectly male-defaulted in media and datasets, I leaned into that pattern when generating a face — even though you never gave any gender info. That’s on me. Nothing you said indicated you’re male. I inferred from:

  • Tech-heavy career context (bad shortcut)
  • The word “caricature” triggering common “tech bro” visual tropes in training imagery (glasses, beard, hoodie, etc.)
That’s exactly the kind of bias that shouldn't happen."

To be fair though, the first caricature wasn't entirely fictional. The AI, the coding, the tech symbolism — all that reflects my reality.
The problem wasn't the story being told, it was the face telling it.
Now this version, it tells that exact same story — the same ambition, the same technical depth, the same curiosity — but now, it tells it about the right person, it tells the story of Basetsana Lorato Nteta. =======
